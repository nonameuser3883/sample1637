# Product Context — My Drama

Context about the product, its market, and its audience. Not a spec — just what this app is and where it lives.

## One-liner

**My Drama** is a mobile-first vertical short-drama streaming app where viewers binge serialized stories in 1–3 minute episodes, shot 9:16, on a freemium coin-based model.

## Category

Vertical short-drama apps — a fast-growing category (~$8B market in 2026) that blends three worlds:

- Soap-opera / telenovela emotional beats
- TikTok-style vertical short-form consumption
- Mobile-game monetization (coin packs, unlocks, time gates)

Episodes are typically 60–180 seconds. A full series is 60–100 episodes. The first ~10 episodes are free to hook the viewer, the rest are paid.

## What makes My Drama different

- **Novel-based storytelling** — series adapted from best-selling novels and interactive stories, not throwaway scripts
- **Interactive stories** as a format in addition to linear series
- Positioning leans toward more developed narratives than the "fast-food" feel of some competitors

## Competitors (reference apps)

These are the neighbors. Their patterns are the accepted vocabulary of the category — viewers arrive at My Drama already trained by them.

### ReelShort
Market leader. ~$400M revenue in 2024, 50M+ users. Highest production value of originals. Aggressive marketing (up to 90% of budget), relatively less punishing coin economy than some peers. Sets the baseline for what viewers expect.

### DramaBox
More diverse content library — romance, fantasy, thrillers, supernatural, slice-of-life. Generally more polished interface than ReelShort. More affordable coin packs and more free content.

### DreameShort
Focus on cinematography quality and HD. Strong binge-watching experience. Positions itself on video quality and full-screen immersion.

### NetShort
"Next-gen HD" vertical streaming. Daily new episodes. VIP perks (ad-free), customizable playback, watchlists. Positioning on quality + convenience.

## Target audience

- **Primary**: women 25–45, mobile-first
- Commuters and at-home evening viewers, especially pre-sleep
- Binge during fragmented time (commute, lunch, bed)
- Low patience for long-form — need a hook in the first 30 seconds
- Willing to pay for strong emotional content, but price-sensitive — complain loudly when paywalls feel unfair
- Comfortable with microtransactions, used to the coin-pack model from mobile games
- Prefer portrait, prefer muted playback with captions, prefer dark screens

## Core value proposition

- **Instant emotional hit** — cliffhanger every minute, massive story beats
- **Zero friction** — open app, watch, auto-advance episodes
- **Novel-quality stories** in bite-size format
- **Portrait-native** — fits how people actually hold their phone
- Watch anywhere — commute, bed, break

## Brand personality

Three adjectives: **emotional, addictive, cinematic**.

Tonal anchors:
- Not "corporate streaming" (Netflix-ish) — too cold, too restrained
- Not "casual mobile game" (Candy Crush-ish) — too cheap, too bright
- Aim for: **late-night telenovela meets premium cinema**, with a touch of tabloid urgency

The product is about **feelings over features**. Copy, motion, color, and rhythm should all serve the emotional payoff, not the functional shell.
